# 🧬 Patient Zero


**A choice-based life simulation game about surviving illness, financial instability, and student life in America.**

---

## 📖 Overview

*Patient Zero* is an interactive, choice-based narrative game that simulates the experience of a college student navigating illness while managing limited finances, academic pressure, and social life.

Players take on the role of a student who becomes seriously ill and must survive three in-game days while balancing:

- ❤️ Health
- 💰 Finances
- 📚 Academic responsibilities

Every decision has consequences, shaping both immediate survival and long-term outcomes. While the game begins as an individual struggle, it gradually reveals how personal health decisions affect others—highlighting broader systemic issues.

---

## 🎯 Motivation

This project was created by a team of computer science, design, and biomedical engineering students to combine technical development with social commentary.

We aimed to explore:

- Healthcare accessibility in the United States
- Financial barriers to treatment
- Real-world consequences of illness for students and low-income individuals

Inspired by ongoing discussions around healthcare policy (including the Affordable Care Act and Medicaid), we wanted to build an experience that is both **immersive and reflective**.

---

## 🎮 Features

- 🎭 Branching, choice-based narrative system  
- ❤️ Health vs 💰 money vs 📚 school balancing mechanics  
- 📉 Consequence-driven gameplay decisions  
- 🕹️ Pixel-style scene transitions for immersion  
- 🧠 Progressive story reveal over three in-game days  
- 🏁 Multiple fail/success states depending on survival

---

## 🛠️ Tech Stack

- **Frontend:** React, TypeScript  
- **Backend / Logic:** Node.js  
- **Styling:** CSS, Pixel Art

---

## 🧩 Gameplay Structure

- Players make decisions each in-game day
- Each decision modifies:
  - Health
  - Financial status
  - Academic progress
- Game ends when:
  - Health reaches zero (illness worsens)
  - Money reaches zero (cannot sustain living costs)
- Successful completion requires surviving all three days

---

## ⚠️ Challenges

- Tight development timeline limited feature expansion
- Some decision paths initially created **looping navigation bugs**
- Lack of consistent Git version control caused difficulty in:
- Tracking changes
- Reversing broken logic
- Not using GitHub to track changes
